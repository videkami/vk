Max Pain

The Max Pain theory in options trading suggests that the stock price tends to move toward the strike price where the most options (both calls and puts) expire worthless.

This price point causes maximum financial loss to options buyers and minimum loss to options writers, the majority of which are market makers, who may influence the market toward that price.

Though not a guarantee, the max pain price is used as a tool to anticipate market movement near options expiration dates.

This script pulls the open interest of all options at every strike price, calculating mark price and the cumulative summation to find the peak value. It repeats this for all available expiration dates.
